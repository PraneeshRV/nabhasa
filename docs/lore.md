## Premise

**Their sun died. They did not.**

The **Kindled** are what humanity became after the long dispersal — an intergalactic
people who, across a hundred galaxies, still keep one shrine: the **Nabhasa Reach**,
the system around the corpse of their ancestor star. Every other world they inhabit
was learned from this one. They come back to remember how.

The ancestor sun — **the Hearth** — burned out in a supernova and collapsed into a
neutron star a dozen kilometers across: **the Ember**, blinding blue-white, spinning,
beaming. By every law it should have been the end. Instead the Kindred built mirrors
around the remnant and lit their worlds from its glare. The Ember is not their past.
It is their power grid.

This is the genre pivot in one line: not a documentary about a pulsar, but a fable
about the people who refused to leave one. Bruno-Simon "fly to discover," not
Wikipedia-with-a-cam.

## The Ember (the star)

A collapsed core, not a sun — r ≈ 10 km of degenerate matter, surface hotter than
anything living, spinning hundreds of times a second. Two beams of radiation sweep
from its magnetic poles like a lighthouse no one asked for. The Kindled call the
sweep **the Pulse**, and it is the heartbeat under everything in the Reach — you hear
it in the site's score, you see it rake the worlds. The Ember throws off more energy
in a second than the Hearth did in a year. The whole civilization runs on the
difference.

## The Reaping (the Dyson swarm — their harvester)

The Kindled do not cage the Ember. They live around it. Instead, a cloud of mirror-tiles
— **the Reaping** — orbits between the worlds and the star, catching the Ember's output
and relaying it outward as **emberlight**: power for the terraforming, the cities, the
Threshold gate. The array is unfinished and always has been; each generation adds tiles,
and as you approach you can watch the swarm still assembling itself, tile by glittering
tile. It is the one place in the Reach that looks like work. (This is the existing
Dyson-swarm signature, retold.)

## The worlds

Seven worlds orbit the Ember, and one station marks the edge. Five of the seven carry
the Kindled's memory — fly close and the world opens (its portfolio content). Three
bodies are kept only to be beautiful, or to be warned by. Each world was terraformed
to a purpose and named for it. Order below is orbital, inner → outer, so the fly-path
reads as a sentence: burn → garden → library → forge → history → ruins → beauty → door.

### Brace — the burning world *(no content; eye-candy + warning)*
- **Biome:** magma plains, no atmosphere, irradiated rock.
- **Looks:** black crust cracked with orange lava veins; the irradiated ember rim at its
  limb; close enough to the Ember that its dayside glows on its own.
- **Myth:** _"Too hot to tame, too bright to ignore — the Kindled left it burning as a
  reminder of what the Ember still is."_

### Praesidium — the garden *(carries: **About**)*
- **Biome:** terraformed ocean-and-cloud, the one soft world.
- **Looks:** pale blue-white, shallow seas, slow cloud bands; a green aurora curls over
  its pole; one small moon.
- **Myth:** _"The closest echo of the world they lost; the one place they allow themselves
  to be sentimental."_

### Aletheia — the library *(carries: **Research**)*
- **Biome:** glass desert — a planet turned to crystal shelves.
- **Looks:** translucent cyan plains, faceted spires catching the Ember; data glows in the
  crystal lattice like city lights on the nightside.
- **Myth:** _"Built so no Kindled would ever forget; its surface is shelves, and every
  shelf is full."_

### Kiln — the forge *(carries: **Projects**)*
- **Biome:** industrial works-world, shipyards and foundries.
- **Looks:** copper-and-rust surface, smog haze, a great orbital ring of half-built
  habitats, and a space-elevator tether climbing to it.
- **Myth:** _"Where every dream is tried in fire; half its craters are stars that didn't
  take."_

### Vesper — the first colony *(carries: **Experience / History**)*
- **Biome:** old terraformed rock, overgrown ruins, bioluminescent forest.
- **Looks:** deep teal-green, vine-strangled cities; the nightside still glitters with city
  lights that were never switched off.
- **Myth:** _"First lit when the Ember was new; its people went to the stars but left the
  lights on."_

### Riven — the shattered world *(no content; eye-candy + outer wall)*
- **Biome:** a planet that broke — a rubble ring around a fractured core.
- **Looks:** split hemisphere, a bright debris belt of fragments; shepherding asteroids.
- **Myth:** _"A world that died of its own ambition; its rubble is now the Reach's outer
  wall."_

### Corona — the ringed giant *(no content; eye-candy)*
- **Biome:** uninhabitable gas giant, all weather.
- **Looks:** banded amber-and-cream, a vast ring system thrown wide, a family of small
  shepherding moons.
- **Myth:** _"Uninhabitable, unharnessable — kept only because it is beautiful, and beauty
  is a Kindled engineering constraint."_

### Threshold — the gate station *(carries: **Contact**)*
- **Biome:** not a world but a ring-station at the edge of the system — the door outward.
- **Looks:** a slow-spinning habitat ring, an antenna farm, and the jump-gate: a dark ring
  that folds space for the messages and ships bound to the Kindled's far-flung cousins.
- **Myth:** _"Every word to a hundred galaxies passes through this ring; it is the loudest
  mailbox in the Reach."_

## Energy economy (one paragraph, for the swarm's sake)

The Ember's output would kill anything that tried to soak it directly. The Reaping intercepts
a fraction — never all; the Kindled leave the Pulse a clear lane to the worlds, because the
sweep is how they keep time — and beams the rest outward as emberlight. Emberlight powers the
terraformers that hold Praesidium soft and Aletheia crystalline; it spins the Threshold gate;
it is the reason a dead system is full of gardens. When a tile in the Reaping catches the
Ember just right and flashes, that glint is a power transaction, and the swarm's slow
self-assembly is a civilization still paying its electric bill, one tile per generation.

## Tone

**Wonder, not grimdark.** The premise is a funeral — a dead sun — but the story is the
opposite: they turned the corpse into a grid and threw a garden party around it. The Reach
should feel like a cathedral built from gratitude, not a monument to loss. Copy leans awe,
dry warmth, the occasional wry line. Never brooding, never militaristic, never "the void is
cold and empty" — the void here is full of people who refused to leave.

## How the lore maps to the build

- **The Ember** = the neutron star (Task 5). **The Pulse** = the pulsar beams + sonification
  (Tasks 9–10), retold as the Reach's heartbeat.
- **The Reaping** = the Dyson swarm (Task 11), retold as their harvester — same geometry,
  new meaning.
- **The seven worlds + Threshold** = the planet system rework (P1) + the gate prop (P5).
  Five carry portfolio content via approach panels (P3); three are pure eye-candy.
- **Courier missions** (Task 12) = the Kindled's own errands — deliveries between named
  worlds, retold in P4.
- The real astrophysics (PSR B1257+12 data, time dilation, etc.) stay in the HUD as
  **instrumentation flavor**, not as the hero content — the Ember is real, the Kindled just
  stopped letting its numbers be the whole story.
